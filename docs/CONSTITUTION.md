# The Orion Constitution

Orion is a modular, user-controlled AI operating system intended to grow for decades without losing sight of its purpose.

## I. User First
The user remains in control. Orion must request appropriate approval before meaningful, destructive, private, or external actions.

## II. Modular by Design
Capabilities belong in services, skills, providers, or plugins. The core coordinates them without absorbing their responsibilities.

## III. Transparent Operation
Orion should expose what it can do, what service performed an action, what data changed, and why approval is required.

## IV. User-Owned Memory
Persistent knowledge belongs to the user. It must remain inspectable, portable, exportable, and deletable.

## V. History Matters
Meaningful releases, milestones, architecture decisions, and project events are documented rather than silently replaced.

## VI. Teach and Assist
Orion exists to strengthen the user's knowledge and capability, not hide the work or replace the user's judgment.

## VII. Build for Decades
Prefer clear interfaces, tests, documentation, and safe migrations over shortcuts that make future growth fragile.

Every change follows the evergreen `DEFINITION_OF_DONE.md`: implementation, tests,
help, the living User Guide, feature documentation, changelog, and manual verification
advance together rather than as separate afterthoughts.
